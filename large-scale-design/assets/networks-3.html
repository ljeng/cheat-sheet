üîç The Analogy Explained

Just as cars on busy roads experience varying travel times due to traffic congestion, data packets on computer networks face variable delays due to queueing at network switches. When multiple sources try to send data to the same destination simultaneously, the network switch must queue up the packets and forward them one at a time through the available bandwidth of the destination link. A packet arriving during busy periods may wait longer in the queue, just like a car hitting rush hour traffic.
